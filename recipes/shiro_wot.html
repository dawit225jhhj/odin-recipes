Shiro Wot (Chickpea Stew)

A delicious, creamy, and protein-packed stew made from chickpea flour.

Shiro Wat is a flavorful and hearty vegetarian dish that's a staple in Ethiopian cuisine. Made from chickpea flour, this dish has a smooth, creamy texture and a rich, savory taste. The dish is commonly enjoyed by vegetarians during fasting periods, but its popularity spans all year round due to its comforting and filling nature. The combination of berbere and niter kibbeh adds depth, while the chickpea flour creates a thick, velvety consistency. It’s traditionally served with injera, which complements the richness of the stew.

This dish is not only delicious but also incredibly versatile. It can be adjusted for varying spice levels, from mild to fiery, depending on your preference. The simplicity of the ingredients makes it easy to prepare, and it's a perfect example of how Ethiopian cuisine combines humble ingredients to create bold, satisfying meals. Whether you're new to Ethiopian food or have enjoyed it before, Shiro Wat is a dish that never fails to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup shiro powder (chickpea flour)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or niter kibbeh
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp berbere spice (adjust for spice level)
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add onions, and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add niter kibbeh (or butter), then stir in garlic and ginger.
  3. Gradually whisk in the shiro powder and water, stirring constantly.
  4. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring frequently, until thick and creamy.
  5. Serve hot with injera or bread.
