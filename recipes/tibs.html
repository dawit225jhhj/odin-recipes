Tibs (Sauteed Meat with Spices)

A flavorful Ethiopian stir-fry dish made with beef or lamb.

Tibs is a flavorful stir-fry dish that’s quick to prepare and packed with vibrant spices. Typically made with beef or lamb, Tibs is seasoned with berbere, garlic, and other aromatic spices that create a savory and slightly spicy taste. It's a popular choice for any time of the day, whether it's a casual meal or part of a larger celebration. The dish is often accompanied by injera, which helps to balance the rich, spicy flavors. The best part of Tibs is its versatility—add vegetables, adjust the heat, or choose your protein to suit your preferences.

What makes Tibs so special is the sizzling preparation of the meat and spices in a hot pan, allowing the flavors to meld together while giving the meat a crispy edge. It's a dish that brings together the traditional flavors of Ethiopia with the convenience of a quick meal. Whether enjoyed as a main course or as part of a larger feast, Tibs is sure to be a crowd-pleaser with its delicious blend of heat and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef or lamb, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tbsp niter kibbeh (or regular butter)
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp berbere spice (or more for extra heat)
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 tsp rosemary or thyme
  • Salt & black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat niter kibbeh in a pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onions and garlic, sauté until soft.
  3. Stir in berbere spice, then add the meat.
  4. Cook for 5-7 minutes until browned, then add tomatoes and rosemary.
  5. Stir well, season with salt and pepper, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with injera or rice.
