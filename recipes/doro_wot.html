Doro Wot (Spicy Chicken Stew)

A rich, spicy, slow-cooked chicken stew that’s the most famous Ethiopian dish.

Doro Wat is one of Ethiopia's most beloved dishes, often served during holidays and special occasions. This rich, spicy chicken stew is packed with bold flavors from berbere spice and niter kibbeh, making it a standout dish in Ethiopian cuisine. The slow-cooked chicken is tender, soaking up the spices, while the hard-boiled eggs add a creamy texture, making each bite an explosion of taste. It’s traditionally served with injera, a spongy flatbread that helps to balance the heat and soak up the flavorful stew.

The preparation of Doro Wat is a labor of love, requiring patience and attention to detail to build the depth of flavor. The key to making an authentic Doro Wat lies in the slow sautéing of onions and spices, allowing the flavors to deepen and meld together. This dish is a celebration of bold spices, and its depth is enhanced by its long cooking process, making it a dish that is both hearty and satisfying. Whether you're cooking for a special occasion or just want to enjoy a comforting meal, Doro Wat is sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 3 tbsp niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 3 boiled eggs (peeled)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, cook the onions on medium heat without oil until soft.
  2. Add niter kibbeh (or butter), then stir in garlic and ginger.
  3. Mix in berbere spice and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Add chicken pieces and coat them in the spice mix.
  5. Pour in chicken broth, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes.
  6. Add boiled eggs and let cook for another 10 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with injera (Ethiopian flatbread).
